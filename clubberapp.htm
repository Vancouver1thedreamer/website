Clubber

A UX case study user research and concept validation for a community social app of creatives

View prototype

Overview

Creatives should meet creatives! An uprising tech startup from Calgary, Canada reached me to come on board in their team to conduct user research, design, validate their innovative concept. The goal was to help the client identify their targeted users, understand their behavior, and the problems they face while using similar apps in the market. Clubber is a mobile social media-based platform that aims to gather and connect creatives from everywhere in the world. Clubber's aim is to establish a community of users with common interests, ideas, hobbies, and talents.

Team's Objective

The objectives we set for our UX research were as follows:
  1. To identify the target audience, understand their behavior and their current processes pertaining to using other community social media apps.
  2. Find User Painpoints using other applications
  3. Identify what motivates people's needs to join online community apps
  4. understand competitor analytics in the space
  5. Design an easy to navigate but engaging interface design for multiple device

Research Process

As a self starter designer, to fully understand and make concrete design decisions for the project, I dedicated two weeks to the research phase. During this phase, I spilt the research & interviews in parts;
  1. Stakholder's interview; Conducting Interviews is one important methodologies & systematic approach to design, we performed both, primary and secondary research. But, before proceeding further with UX research, I interviewed the stakeholders. It was important to understand how they perceived their product, target audience, and the problems they hoped their app would solve for its users.
  2. User interview; As part of our primary research, we conducted user interviews. We interviewed 20 individuals who were identical to the user personas we created. The purpose was to have a qualitative perspective of the experiences of actual users who used payments apps in their day to day activities. It helped us to understand exactly what users look for when they use a payments app, their primary pain points and what we can do to make their experiences better.
  3. Competitve analytics; This part
  1. Empathy mapping; The empathy map created served as a pictorial roadmap to fully comprehend and help synthesize our observations from the research phase. It was essentially important I analyze and put myself in the user's environment. The empathy map helped capture user behavior, establish the user personas & story, and help with additional insight into the user needs.
  2. Survey To gather data, I created a survey with a google form and sent them out for potential users to fill, platforms such as discord were very helpful to distribute and conduct the survey. The survey form was also shared in my university department group, which produced 45% of The Survey data gathered. This tool served as means to get insights into individual views and experiences to aid the design decisions.
  3. Personas The personas were derived from the rounds of research and interviews. We focused on creating goal-directed user personas that would help us identify design problems and devise product strategies. It provided a clear understanding of the user behavior and who the product was being created for.
